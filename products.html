Wooden Doors (Timber Doors)

Material: Teak, Sal, Sheesham, Pine, Engineered wood, MDF

Features:
Classic & aesthetic look
Durable and strong (especially hardwoods)
Can be carved, polished, or painted

Common Uses:
Main entrance doors
Bedroom and indoor doors

Pros:
Good insulation (sound & temperature)
Traditional and elegant

Cons:
Expensive (especially solid wood)
Can warp in moisture without proper treatment

Popular Brands: Greenply, Century Doors, Duro, Kitply

×

Steel Doors

Material: Galvanized steel, powder-coated steel, sometimes with honeycomb or PU core

Features:
High strength and security
Weather-resistant
Fire-resistant options available

Common Uses:
Main entrance, security doors, commercial buildings

Pros:
Low maintenance
Affordable
Termite-proof and corrosion-resistant (if coated)

Cons:
Less aesthetic than wood
Heavier

Popular Brands: Godrej, Tata Pravesh, Dorma, Sainik

×

Glass Doors

Material: Tempered glass, frosted glass, laminated glass with aluminum or wooden frames

Features:
Sleek and modern
Transparent or translucent for light passage
Available as sliding, hinged, or frameless

Common Uses:
Bathrooms, balconies, offices, shower partitions

Pros:
Elegant and space-enhancing
Good for visual openness

Cons:
Needs frequent cleaning
Not ideal for high-security zones (unless reinforced)

Popular Brands: Saint-Gobain, Modiguard, AIS Glass
